FedEx + Accenture: A Powerful Partnership

FedEx is a 50-year-old Fortune 100 company headquartered in Memphis, Tennessee, United States. It offers a wide range of services in transportation, e-commerce, and business operations, supporting global trade and supply chain needs.

With a strong culture of innovation, FedEx leverages data from every delivery to enhance tracking, visibility, and customer satisfaction. The company is also committed to sustainability and community support, constantly seeking to minimize its environmental impact while giving back to society.

FedEx employs over 400,000 team members and connects 99% of the world's GDP, underscoring its massive influence on global logistics. The physical network‚Äîplanes, trucks, and people‚Äîbuilt over five decades has made FedEx one of the most impactful logistics organizations in the world.

Looking to the Future

As it enters its next 50 years, FedEx is shifting focus to technology and data-driven innovation, aiming to "make supply chains smarter for everyone." A critical part of this transformation is its strategic collaboration with Accenture.

Partnership with Accenture

This partnership began with strategy and consulting to help establish a new Chief Digital and Information Officer (CDIO) organization. It evolved into a major Managed Services contract for modernizing technology delivery, and has now advanced into a Journey-to-Cloud transformation program.

The goal is to transition FedEx from a traditional logistics company into a technology-led enterprise, with enhanced productivity, faster delivery, and reduced operational costs.

Scale & Impact

The seven-year Managed Services contract includes system development, enhancement, support, and maintenance‚Äîconsolidating services once handled by multiple vendors. Over 7,000 professionals are involved in this initiative, aiming to drive a 33% cost saving.

Global delivery hubs in Memphis and Amsterdam, supported by centers in India and the Philippines, ensure worldwide coverage. This partnership marks a pivotal evolution in FedEx‚Äôs mission‚Äîredefining itself as a leader in both logistics and digital innovation.

Inclusion and Diversity at Accenture

Our Commitment

At Accenture, our commitment to inclusion and diversity begins with our leadership and is sustained by the efforts of all our people. We strive to advance a culture of equality where everyone feels they belong and can thrive.

How We Foster a Culture of Equality

We embrace a wide view of diversity and believe no one should be discriminated against based on age, disability, race and ethnicity, gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, or religion. To support our people, we offer:

  • Specialized training and mentoring programs
  • Networking support and flexible work arrangements
  • Mental health resources and equal benefits to same-sex couples where permitted
  • Accessible technologies and resources for persons with disabilities

Gender Equality

We believe the future workforce is an equal one. We're committed to achieving gender balance by 2025. Currently, 48% of our global workforce are women, with 45% representation on our board of directors and 26% on our global management committee.

Cross-Cultural Diversity

Embracing cultural diversity enhances our ability to innovate and support our global clients. Understanding and leveraging the diversity of multiple cultures helps us be One Accenture.

Neurodiversity

Neurodiversity describes natural variations in the human brain and relates to differences in how individuals think, learn, process, and behave. We're on a journey to create a neuroinclusive working environment that supports neurodivergent individuals to thrive.

Disability Inclusion

We're creating an accessible, inclusive environment where all our people can belong and thrive. Persons with disabilities at Accenture have access to the latest technology, resources, and training for a barrier-free workplace.
